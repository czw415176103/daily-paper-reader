Abstract 

Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for coupled multiphysics systems suffer systematic accuracy degradation as inter-equation coupling strengthens. We provide a theoretical explanation for this phenomenon through neural tangent kernel (NTK) analysis: for linearly coupled systems, we prove that the standard NTKвАЩs spectral radius grows as ќ©( ќ≥2) with coupling strength ќ≥,shrinking the stable learning rate, while block-diagonal GaussвАУNewton (GN) preconditioning yields a preconditioned NTK KP = JH +JвК§ (where H is the block-diagonal GN Hessian) whose spectral radius is bounded by S (S = number of networks), independent of ќ≥. We verify the ќ©( ќ≥2) growth numerically across symmetric, asymmetric, and nonlinear coupled PDE systems, and confirm ќїmax (KP ) = S with equality in all cases. Combining the Kronecker-preconditioned optimizer SOAP with inverse-gradient-norm loss balancing (SOAP+GN) yields coupling-robust accuracy: across 234 experiments spanning three 1D systems of increasing nonlinearity and a 2D electroosmotic flow benchmark, SOAP+GN maintains final-epoch L2 degradation вЙ§ 1.1√Ч

(ratio of strong- to weak-coupling error) even as coupling parameters vary over one to two orders of magnitude, compared with > 10 2√Ч for Adam+GN. SOAP+GN further scales to a 2D, 6-PDE electroosmotic flow system at EDL-resolved conditionsвАФa regime that all prior PINN electrokinetics studies have avoided through simplified physicsвАФwhere Adam+GN fails entirely (L2 > 0.9). 

# 1 Introduction 

Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) [Raissi et al., 2019] embed PDE residuals into neural network loss functions, enabling mesh-free solutions. While PINNs have achieved broad success on single-equation problems [Karniadakis et al., 2021, Wang et al., 2022, Hao et al., 2024], coupled multiphysics systemsвАФwhere multiple PDEs interact through shared variablesвАФremain challenging [Sun et al., 2024]. A fundamental difficulty is that coupled PDE residuals compete for optimizer capacity. Each loss termвАЩs gradient may conflict with others, and relative magnitudes depend on coupling strength.

As coupling intensifies, this imbalance worsens, causing accuracy degradation far beyond the single-equation setting. Despite extensive work on adaptive weightingвАФboth global loss balancing [Wang et al., 2021] and local pointwise schemes [McClenny and Braga-Neto, 2023, Anagnostopoulos et al., 2024]вАФand characterization of PINN failure modes in single-equation settings [Krishnapriyan et al., 2021, Wang et al., 2022], no prior work has systematically measured how accuracy scales with coupling strength across multiple systems, nor provided a theoretical explanation for why preconditioning resolves this challenge. 

Contributions. (1) We prove that for any linearly coupled system, the standard NTKвАЩs spectral radius grows as ќ©( ќ≥2) with coupling strength, while block-diagonal GN preconditioning bounds the preconditioned NTK at S (number of networks) independent of coupling, and verify these predictions numerically across four benchmark systems (Sections 4 and 5). (2) Through a 234-experiment factorial comparison (2 optimizers √Ч 3 balancing schemes √Ч 4 systems), we show that SOAP+GN is the only method achieving coupling degradation вЙ§ 1.1√Ч across all regimes. (3) We demonstrate SOAP+GN on a 2D, 6-PDE electroosmotic flow system that directly resolves the electric double layer (EDL)вАФa regime all prior PINN electrokinetics studies have avoided through simplified physics.

# 8 Discussion 

TheoryвАУexperiment connection. The NTK analysis (Section 4) provides three levels of theoret-ical support for the empirical results. 

Linear coupling (formal). For systems where coupling enters the Jacobian as Ji,ќЄ k = ќ≥ C ik ,Theorem 1 proves ќїmax (K) = ќ©( ќ≥2) and Theorem 2 proves ќїmax (KP ) вЙ§ S, independent of ќ≥. Both bounds are verified numerically with the predicted scaling (Figure 1 and Table 3). 

Nonlinear coupling (heuristic). For NP+P, the ќ©( ќ≥2) scaling does not hold at initialization (Table 3), but an effective spectral growth emerges during training as the solution develops O(1 /ќµ )gradients (Remark 2). The GN bound ќїmax (KP ) вЙ§ S remains exact throughout training (Figure 2). 

SOAP as approximate GN (structural). SOAPвАЩs layer-wise Kronecker preconditioning is auto-matically network-wise block-diagonal in the segregated-network setting (Proposition 1), yielding 

ќїmax (KSOAP  

> P

) вЙ§ SL вАФa looser but still ќ≥-independent bound. Closing the gap between SL and the network-wise bound S remains open; the empirical coupling robustness of SOAP is additionally supported by the SOAPвАУNewton connection of Wang et al. [2025]. 

Why Adam does not resolve coupling. As analyzed in Section 4, AdamвАЩs diagonal preconditioner ( вЙИ diag (H)вИТ1) cannot compensate for the ќ©( ќ≥2) spectral growth that originates from cross-network Jacobian blocks (Equation (15)), because these block-level interactions are invisible to per-parameter scaling. This is reflected empirically: at strong coupling, SOAP (unbalanced) improves over Adam by 41вАУ143 √Ч in absolute L2 (Table 5). 

Limitations. (1) The GD spectral bound (Theorem 1) applies only to linearly coupled systems; for nonlinearly coupled systems such as NP+P, we provide a heuristic mechanism (Remark 2) but not a formal proof of training-time spectral growth. (2) The SOAP spectral bound SL (Proposition 1) is looser than the exact GN bound S; closing this gap is an open problem. 

Computational cost. SOAP incurs вИЉ1.6√Ч wall-clock overhead per epoch compared to Adam due to Kronecker factor maintenance. Given the 40вАУ100 √Ч accuracy improvement, this is modest. 

# 9 Conclusion 

We have provided both theoretical and empirical evidence that Kronecker-preconditioned opti-mization achieves coupling-robust accuracy in multiphysics PINNs. The NTK analysis reveals the mechanism: for linearly coupled systems, the standard NTKвАЩs spectral radius grows as ќ©( ќ≥2) with coupling strength, while block-diagonal GN preconditioning bounds the preconditioned NTK at 

S (number of networks)вАФindependent of coupling, PDE structure, and network parameterization. This spectral bound characterizes the conditioning of linearized residual dynamics; while not a global convergence guarantee, the qualitative contrast ќЈGD max = O(1 /ќ≥ 2) versus ќЈGN max = O(1) correctly predicts the empirical coupling robustness. Numerical verification confirms ќїmax (KP ) = S across 27 symmetric-model experiments and 4 benchmark systems, while ќїmax (K) spans 4 orders of magnitude. 14 Empirically, SOAP+GN achieves coupling degradation of 0 .9вАУ1 .1√Ч across three 1D systems and solves a 2D, 6-PDE EDL-resolved electroosmotic flow problemвАФa regime that all prior PINN electrokinetics studies avoidedвАФachieving L2 = 1 .3√Ч10 вИТ3 at ќµ = 0 .2 where Adam+GN fails entirely.
